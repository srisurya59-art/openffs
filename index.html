What this is

OpenFFS is being built as an open engineering knowledge platform for Fitness-for-Service assessment — one that shows a junior engineer the reasoning while a senior engineer records it.

It is in development. The upload and assessment function that previously appeared on this page has been taken offline.

Why it is offline

An independent audit of the codebase found no calculation that had been verified against API 579-1/ASME FFS-1, and several that were incorrect. Until a calculation reproduces a published worked example and a named engineer signs for it, its output cannot be relied on — and a clean-looking report makes a wrong number harder to catch, not easier.

Fitness-for-Service results feed run, repair and replace decisions on pressure equipment. That is not a place to publish work in progress.

Two rules, not negotiable

  1. No equation enters the software from memory. Every equation, coefficient, limit and acceptance criterion is transcribed from the licensed standard by a qualified engineer and carries its clause citation in the code.
  2. Unverified modules refuse to run. A module that has not reproduced a published worked example raises an error instead of returning a number. The test suite enforces this.

Path back to service

  • Part 4 Level 1 rewritten against the standard text, with every value cited to its clause
  • Published worked examples reproduced; verification register signed
  • Applicability limits enforced, so out-of-scope inputs are refused rather than calculated
  • Unit-system enforcement and a full regression suite
  • Independent review by a second senior engineer

No release date is claimed. The tool returns when the verification dossier exists, and not before.

Engineering critique and offers of independent review are welcome and valued.
